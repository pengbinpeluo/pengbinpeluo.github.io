Biography

I am a Research Assistant Professor at Department of Natural Resources and Environmental Sciences (NRES) and Research Scientist at Institute for Sustainability, Energy, and Environment (ISEE) of University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. My primary research interests are hydrological and crop modeling in the framework of earth system models, remote sensing of soil moisture and crop productivity, and data-model integration. The overarching goal of my research is to deepen our understanding of land-atmosphere interactions, terrestrial carbon-water-nutrient cycles, and human impacts on earth systems under climate change and land use intensification. The societal drivers for these research interests include adaptation of climate change, mitigation of hydrometeorological and hydroclimatological disasters like droughts and floods, as well as ensuring of water, energy and food securities and environmental sustainability. Over the past years, I have been focusing on the U.S. Midwestern agroecosystems.
